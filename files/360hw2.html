For this assignment, 3 different data sets are received from the Electronic Data Delivery System , covering the years between 2014-2019 and containing information on a monthly basis. And also a fourth data set is created connected to both of the data sets received by manipulating these data. As a result, a study is conducted examining the relationships of these data and including visual analysis.

Aim of the Study

There are many factors affecting the foreign trade policies and foreign trade balances of states. Some of these factors can be listed as politically based and some are economically based. If we consider the economy-based factors, the exchange rate may be the first factor that comes to mind. According to the economic theory, if the exchange rate increases, exports increase, imports decrease and thus net exports increase. In this case, the foreign trade balance improves. Otherwise, the relationship proceeds in the opposite direction. In this context this study aims to observe,whether the foreign trade balance of Turkey is affected by the foreign exchange rate changes between 2014 and 2019 in Turkey as in the theory . So the main question of the study is; Does the change in the exchange rate have an effect on total imports, total exports, and thus on the balance of net exports and foreign economy, as theoretically developed?

Visualisations and Analyses of Data

When the graph is examined in a simple way, it can be seen that exports are generally below the imports in the given time period, therefore net exports are at negative values.Also,it can be deduced that the exchange rate has always been in an increasing trend over the years, except for monthly fluctuations.If the relative data movements are compared, it is seen that the exchange rate and exports and net exports are moving in the same direction , whereas import data show an opposite movement to these data.

In order to obtain more detailed information about the data, it may be more beneficial to apply different visualization and analysis methods at this stage. For example, it would be a logical choice to take a look at the histogram graphs in order to obtain the information about the mobility of each data by years and at what intervals it travels. Histograms

When the distribution of the data in the ranges in the histograms is examined, it can be observed that the imports tend to be more regularly in the ranges especially in 2015 and 2016 , other data are relatively more prone to cluster at certain points.On the other hand, one of the striking data in the analysis is that the difference between the max and min value of the exchange rate in 2018 is much higher than in other years. This difference may indicate that there were sharp fluctuations in the money market during the year, which may be influenced by various factors.Through histograms, information about the distribution trends of the data in the intervals or the width of the gap can be obtained, but examining the data on a single boxplot to compare them with each other will provide better information.

Boxplot

When the boxplots are examined, the relationship between the data can be seen as comparative over the years. In this analysis, the fact that the export values are always below the import and therefore the net export values are always at the negative, the rapid increase in the exchange rate and the wide range of changes in 2018 are some of the interpretations that stand out at first glance.In addition, the fact that import data are relatively decreased with the increase in exchange rate and that exports tend to increase gradually, which can be observed more easily in recent years, supports the theory.

Pairwise Comparisons

In order to examine the mobility of the data against the exchange rate, drawing their distribution graphs and seeing how their general trends will be will be a good guide for the next step, the correlation phase, where it will analyze to what extent their relationships affect each other.In the first graph, it can be seen that exports are in an upward trend with the increase in exchange rates, and in the second, imports have a decreasing tendency in response to this situation. However, looking at the slopes of the lines, it is seen that the line in the import graph is more horizontal. This shows that imports are not affected by changes in exchange rates as strongly as exports. On the other hand, the line in the graph of net exports has a higher slope than the others, indicating that it is the data most affected by the exchange rate increase. Now conducting correlation analysis to support these interpretations reveals numerical data to support this thesis.

Conclusion

When each of the visuals are examined in detail and especially when the correlation data is taken into account, it can be easily concluded that the relationship between the data is as in the general economic theory mentioned at the beginning and there is not a remarkable mobility that supports the contrary.However, since the Ceteris Paribus principle of the economy is not valid in real data and many different factors are in place, although correlation basically reflects the correct and inverse ratio relations of the data, at some points these relations may not be at the expected strength.For example, while it can be seen that the exchange rate and the import are inversely proportional, it can be clearly understood that this ratio is weaker than the exchange rate and export relationship. So, it means that exchange rates have a major influence on exports than in imports in Turkey. In this case, there must be other more important factors affecting imports. This knowledge can open the door to a new research topic.In addition, the fact that the direct relationship between net exports and exchange rate is visible and the exchange rate has increased significantly over the years, the fact that the net export data is always negative, that is, our foreign trade deficit can be attributed to the fact that the rise in the exchange rate does not have the necessary negative effect on imports.In line with this study, the following results can be reached; The period of time covered in turkey that moves in the direction of the data correlating but supports the general economic theory, economic or political-based than many other variables that might play a role these relations. Examining why the exchange rate and import relationship does not function at the desired strength and what the factors that affect it are examined and when necessary improvement studies are made on the factors, it becomes easier to take steps to close the foreign trade deficit and improve the foreign trade balance.
